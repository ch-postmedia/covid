Alberta Premier Jason Kenney is isolating at home after one of his cabinet ministers tested positive for COVID-19. A spokesman for Kenney says the premier went into self-isolation after learning that Municipal Affairs Minister Tracy Allard was infected.
Some developments across the country you may have missed last night
B.C. health officials are reporting 203 new COVID-19 infections, the highest daily number so far. There are now 1,766 active cases, including 70 people in hospital. Cases have been reported at five more care facilities around Metro Vancouver, while outbreaks have been declared over at five other sites.
Quebec is reporting 1,072 new cases of COVID-19 and 19 additional deaths attributed to the novel coronavirus. The province says there are four public long-term care homes and nine private seniors residences where more than 25 per cent of residents have COVID-19. Montreal is the epicentre of the virus in Canada, with almost 40,000 cases.
Ontario is reporting 790 new cases of COVID-19 today and nine new deaths. In total, 260 people are hospitalized, including 71 in intensive care. The province says 49 people are on ventilators.
Visitor restrictions, which were loosened in the summer after months of effective lockdown, have been reimposed at seniors facilities across Saskatchewan as COVID-19 transmission rises. Visits are allowed only for “compassionate reasons.”
More than two dozen flights carrying COVID-19-infected passengers landed at Toronto Pearson International airport during the past two weeks. Online records posted by Health Canada show that since Oct. 5, 16 international and nine domestic flights touched down at YYZ carrying people with COVID infections.
